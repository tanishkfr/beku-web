A pass over the full landing page — ten sections, one FLIP wordmark, two accordions, a 3D tilt card, a scroll-driven word reveal, and a procedurally generated ambient soundscape — read through three practitioners' lenses, weighted for a brand that wants to be felt, not noticed.

Overall

The motion is clearly intentional — every animation has a reason, nothing fires on high-frequency actions, and the accessibility baseline is among the best I've seen in a marketing page. The standout moments (FLIP wordmark, word-by-word scroll reveal, book spines rising, stars popping in) are genuinely distinctive. The gap is completion: accordion exits are unfinished mirrors of their enters, and the two most important text moments — the hero tagline and the quote cascade — stop one ingredient short of the materialising quality that would make them unmistakable. Fix the exits in ten minutes. Add blur to those two text moments in five. Then consider whether the shared EASE token deserves more personality than browser-default ease.

Important Jhey Jakub

Two narrative text moments stop one ingredient short

WhatThe hero tagline ("The Bangalore you wish there were more of") and the Reviews quote blockquotes animate with opacity + translateY but no blur. The materialising quality that makes premium text moments feel distinct — where words emerge from a soft focus — is missing from the two most important text moments on the page.

Why it mattersJhey's lens: the scroll reveal proves the site can think inventively about text motion. The hero tagline and quote cascade are the narrative anchors of the page. Adding filter: blur(6px) to the initial state and blur(0) to the animate state gives them the same materialising quality that makes the word-by-word reveal feel earned — without repeating the technique.

Recommended motionAdd filter: blur(6px) to the hero tagline fade(1.0) call and to the Reviews blockquote animate-in. Use cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) instead of the current EASE token (which is browser-default ease). Duration 0.8s on the tagline, keep 0.7s on the quotes.

Important Jakub

Accordion exits mirror their enters exactly

WhatBoth the Food menu accordion and the Events quirk reveal use exit={{ height: 0, opacity: 0 }} with the same duration as the enter (0.32s). The enter and exit are symmetric — identical travel, identical speed. When you collapse a row, it takes as long to disappear as it did to appear.

Why it mattersJakub's rule: exits should always be subtler than enters — smaller movement, faster duration. The user's attention is already moving on; a slow exit holds them in place unnecessarily. An enter that takes 320ms should exit in 180–200ms.

Recommended motionAdd a per-state transition to the exit: exit={{ height: 0, opacity: 0, transition: { height: { duration: 0.18, ease: [0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1] }, opacity: { duration: 0.12, ease: [0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1] } } }. The enter stays at 320ms; the exit sharpens to 180ms.
