beku-web — Navbar & Hero Motion + Typography

Premium café / bookstore / bakery landing page, Bangalore. Next.js 16 App Router, Framer Motion 12, Tailwind v4. Audit scope: navbar scroll behaviour, hero entrance choreography, site-wide typography fit.

OVERALL

Strong motion intention throughout — the cinematic hero entrance, the FLIP wordmark transition, the scroll-driven word reveal in Story, and the text scramble on "Beku." are all distinctive and brand-appropriate. The gaps live at the operational layer: the navbar background transition runs at 700ms (firmly in the sluggish zone), the backdropFilter change is excluded from the transition and snaps, and every section's content uses identical translateY + opacity entrances regardless of element type. Typography fit is strong — Cormorant Garamond / Plus Jakarta Sans / DM Mono is an appropriate, considered three-font system for a premium bookstore-café. The main underuse: Cormorant appears exclusively italic across all nine sections; the Roman upright is never touched.

--font-cormorant

APT

Legitimate serif usage — bookstore-café is one of the few briefs where heritage serif is genuinely justified, not defaulted. Not Fraunces or Instrument Serif (the banned LLM defaults). Italic form is distinctive. Concern: the Roman upright is never used across all 9 sections.

--font-dm-sans

Plus Jakarta Sans

Body · Navigation · Prices · Metadata

APT

Humanist, warm, not Inter. Good weight range. Concerns: (1) variable named --font-dm-sans but actually Plus Jakarta Sans — naming mismatch. (2) Weight 300 used almost universally; the weight range is underutilised.

--font-stamp / --font-mono

DM Mono

Stamp labels · Terminal · Data

APT

Clinical precision creates deliberate contrast with the warmth of Cormorant. The vibe-coding additions (terminal card, cursor blink) double its role and strengthen the system tension rather than muddying it.

Implementation concerns

  • Cormorant is always italic. Every editorial line, every display heading, every section intro across all 9 sections has fontStyle: "italic". The Roman upright is equally beautiful and would add typographic register. Best candidates for upright: Visit "Beku." (line 47), "Just beyond the traffic." (line 202) — the exhibition-label format reads stronger upright. Visit.tsx:47, Visit.tsx:202
  • Plus Jakarta Sans weight 300 almost everywhere. All body copy — Reviews fragments, Events details, Visit address, Food descriptions — is weight 300. Thin body weight is an AI design tell that flattens hierarchy and reduces readability. Two instances were fixed this session (Books note and Story closer, both → 400). More remain: Reviews.tsx:132, Events.tsx:148, Visit.tsx:119
  • No intermediate type tier. The hierarchy jumps from huge Cormorant italic → tiny mono labels (0.5rem) → body weight 300. There is no subheading tier between display and body. Events event names and Books author attribution feel undifferentiated from body copy. Events.tsx:132, Books.tsx:205
  • Variable naming mismatch. --font-dm-sans resolves to Plus Jakarta Sans via next/font. Low risk functionally but will confuse future maintainers. Rename to --font-jakarta. app/globals.css

Jakub Krehel — Production Polish Primary

Concern — Signature moments are polished; the operational layer lets them down.

What's working well

  • FLIP wordmark — layoutId="beku-wordmark" with matching fontWeight: 400 and letterSpacing: "0.04em" on both hero h1 and navbar. No snap on morph. Arrival.tsx:79, Navbar.tsx:104
  • Exit subtler than enter — mobile menu links exit at y: 8 vs enter at y: 20. Jakub principle: exits don't need the same attention as enters. Navbar.tsx:235
  • Hero entrance is structured narrative, not stagger template — bg → label → wordmark → tagline → card → cue → strip is a deliberate sequence. Once-per-visit cinematic timing is appropriate.
  • Text scramble fires once and settles — module-level bekuScramblePlayed flag prevents re-animation on scroll-back. Arrival.tsx:19

Issues to address

ImportantJakub · Emil

Navbar background transition runs at 700ms — firmly in the sluggish zone

Whattransition: "background-color 700ms ease, border-color 700ms ease" — the navbar bg takes 700ms to become opaque after the user scrolls past 60px. Navbar.tsx:86

Why it mattersThis transition fires on every page visit. At 700ms the navbar's appearance visibly lags behind the scroll action. If users notice the animation itself rather than the result, it is too slow.

RecommendedChange to 420ms ease. Smooth enough to feel premium; fast enough to feel responsive. Also add backdrop-filter to this string (see Finding 2).

ImportantJakub

backdrop-filter excluded from transition — snaps from none to blur(16px)

WhatThe transition string covers only background-color and border-color. When isLight flips, backdropFilter: "blur(16px)" appears instantly. The frosted glass effect snaps on. Navbar.tsx:84–86

Why it mattersBlur should materialise, not snap. An unsmoothed backdrop-filter change reads as a glitch even when the background-color transition is correctly eased.

Recommended"background-color 420ms ease, border-color 420ms ease, backdrop-filter 420ms ease, -webkit-backdrop-filter 420ms ease". The browser interpolates blur from 0px to 16px over the same curve.

ImportantJakub

Uniform translateY + opacity on every element in every section

WhatEvery section uses { opacity: 0, y: 6–12 } → { opacity: 1, y: 0 } for everything: headlines, body copy, images, fragments, cards. All nine sections. Same animation. Story.tsx:53, Events.tsx:31, Reviews.tsx:35, Visit.tsx:18

Why it mattersAnti-pattern: uniform fade-in on every element. When every distinct component type shares the same enter animation, it reads as template output. Context should drive timing and easing.

RecommendedImages: opacity fade only, no Y translate. Editorial quotes: slower ease-out with left-origin slide. Fragment lists: staggered opacity only. Cards: scale-in with a spring. Reserve translateY for primary narrative elements where vertical emergence carries meaning.

Fix the first two immediately; the third requires per-section choreography.

Jhey Tompkins — Experimentation & Delight Secondary

Strong — Genuine signature moments. More creative territory in section transitions.

What's working well

  • Text scramble on "Beku." — character-randomising before settling. Not decoration. The name emerges. The 680ms delay before scrambling matches the h1 fade-in so users see motion only when the element is visible. Arrival.tsx:20–50
  • Scroll-driven word reveal in Story — direct DOM writes at 350ms CSS per-word. Purposeful: scroll position IS the experience. Story.tsx:27–36
  • Terminal cursor blink on location label — step-end timing is authentically terminal, not decorative. Vibe-coding additions are coherent with each other. globals.css:117, Arrival.tsx:151
  • Books card 3D tilt on mouse — useMotionValue + useSpring, no React re-renders. Tactile without being loud. Books.tsx:34–38

Opportunities

  • Spring on the FLIP wordmark. Currently uses default ease. A type: "spring", stiffness: 200, damping: 26 spring gives the transition physical weight — the wordmark "drops into" the navbar. Two-line change, high impact. Arrival.tsx:79
  • Cormorant Roman on word reveal. Unrevealed words in italic, revealed words flipping to Roman upright — present tense vs. past tense. Makes the reveal semantically meaningful, not just visual. Story.tsx:76
  • Section-identity enters. Space could expand from the photograph outward. Reviews fragments already have distinct horizontal offsets — they could enter along those offset axes. Make the enter match what the section IS.

Through Jhey's lens: The scramble and word-reveal are exactly "make it specific to this thing." The rest of the page plays it safe after that opening statement. The section-to-section transitions are a blank canvas — spring on the FLIP and section-identity enters are the highest-impact Jhey-style additions available.

Emil Kowalski — Restraint & Speed Selective

Concern — Two specific violations at the nav layer. Hero long durations are a false positive for Emil.

What's working well

  • Story word reveal uses direct DOM writes (span.style.opacity, span.style.transform), not useState — avoids React tree re-renders on every scroll event. Emil's approach for high-frequency updates. Story.tsx:33–35
  • prefersReduced gates every animation — globally in globals.css and per-component. The !important global rule covers .beku-cursor without a separate override. globals.css:117
  • Mobile overlay at 280ms, desktop nav hover at 280ms — both within Emil's deliberate zone. Navbar.tsx:137, 211

Issues to address

ImportantEmil

Mobile nav link hover animates letter-spacing — a layout-triggering property

WhatonMouseEnter sets letterSpacing = "-0.01em"; onMouseLeave resets to "0em". Letter-spacing is a layout-triggering property — it forces text relayout and shifts characters. Navbar.tsx:252–260

Why it mattersOn mobile, layout recalculation is more expensive. The other nav links may visibly shift on hover. Emil's rule: never animate layout-triggering properties. The existing opacity 220ms ease transition already provides clear hover feedback.

RecommendedRemove the letterSpacing assignments from both handlers. The opacity change (0.72 → 1) is sufficient. If more feedback is needed, add a color transition only.
