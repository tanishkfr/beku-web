# 03 — PHOTOGRAPHY
## Principles · Shot List · Placeholder Specifications

---

## The Single Non-Negotiable Rule

**No photograph of Beku should ever feel staged.**

No posed smiles. No perfect food styling. No lifestyle photography. No marble surfaces with garnish. No overhead flatlays designed for the grid.

Every photograph should feel quietly observed — as if someone happened to look up and take it.

---

## The Source of Light

All Beku photography originates from one of three natural light sources:

1. **Rain tree filtered light** — dappled, moving, green-cast, never direct. The dominant light condition of the outdoor seating area. When this light appears, it is always from the side or above, never frontal. Shadows move.

2. **Bungalow window light** — warm, directional, from old single-pane windows set in thick plaster walls. Creates one bright side and one shadowed side. Not diffused. Specific.

3. **Interior lamp light** — warm, low, amber. Used only indoors at dusk or evening. Never as the primary light in a food photograph.

**Rule:** If the light source cannot be named as one of these three, the photograph does not belong at Beku.

---

## Five Composition Rules

1. **Something is always present.** No empty spaces. Every frame contains a person, a cup, a book, food — some evidence of life.

2. **People are never looking at the camera.** People are reading, talking, looking at a menu, listening. The camera is not invited into the moment. It arrived uninvited.

3. **The photograph is cropped from life, not composed for the shot.** The subject is not centred. The frame is slightly imperfect. Something is at the edge.

4. **Close photographs are allowed to be uncomfortably close.** The Mysore Pak Croissant shot should fill the frame before you understand what it is.

5. **No lifestyle photography.** A lifestyle photograph shows how you could feel at Beku. Beku photography shows Beku itself — and lets the visitor feel whatever they feel.

## Shot List

### Arrival
*What it must show:* The approach. The moment before you enter.

- The bungalow gate from outside. Late morning. Rain tree light on old stone path.
- The gate should be slightly ajar. Not fully open, not closed.
- No people in frame. The frame should feel like someone walked in just before the photograph.
- Colour: Green-cast from tree canopy. Warm base. Natural, not adjusted.

---

### Story
*What it must show:* A person who belongs here.

- A staff member or the owner near the bookshelves. Not looking at the camera.
- Medium shot. Bungalow window light from one side creating depth.
- They are doing something — shelving a book, making coffee, reading.
- No uniform. No name tag visible. Just a person in the space.

---

### Space
*What it must show:* Where you would sit.

- The outdoor seating under the rain tree looking up through the canopy.
- OR: The verandah with one person reading — far enough away to be private.
- OR: The indoor seating between bookshelves, afternoon light on the table.
- Never an overhead shot. Never a wide empty establishing shot.

---

### Food
*What it must show:* The specific item, before the viewer has named it.

**The Mysore Pak Croissant:**
- Shot close. On a dark ceramic plate. Side light only.
- You see the layers of pastry and the Mysore Pak filling before your brain identifies the form.
- The plate is already at the table. It arrived before the photograph.

**The coffee:**
- The steel tumbler. Not a ceramic cup. Not a paper cup.
- Steam if it is morning. Condensation if it is cold.
- Close enough to feel the warmth or the cold.

---

### Books
*What it must show:* Discovery.

- A close shot of book spines on a shelf. Afternoon light.
- One handmade bookmark tucked between two books, protruding slightly.
- A genre shelf marker visible at the edge of frame.
- OR: A single book face-out on a display stand, the staff note card visible behind it, slightly out of focus.

---

### Reviews / Community
*What it must show:* People staying longer than they planned.

- Two people at a table. Neither looking at the camera.
- Warm afternoon indoor light. Possibly the first floor library.
- Something on the table — books, cups, a receipt. The evidence of time passing.
- Medium shot, slightly from the side.

---

### Visit
*What it must show:* Memory, not direction.*

- The bungalow from across the street, partly obscured by the rain tree. Dusk light.
- OR: The view from the outdoor seating looking toward the bungalow — as if this is what you remember when you think about the place.

---

## Placeholder Specifications

Until real photography exists, every placeholder must anticipate the correct image. When building placeholders:

| Section | Light temperature | Primary cast | Secondary cast | Mood |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arrival | Warm amber | Green (canopy) | — | Anticipation |
| Story | Warm window | Directional side | Cool shadow | Presence |
| Space | Warm golden | Green-amber | — | Stillness |
| Food | Warm side light | Deep shadow | Amber | Appetite |
| Books | Warm afternoon | Amber shelf | — | Discovery |
| Reviews | Warm interior | Amber lamp | — | Belonging |
| Visit | Warm dusk | Blue sky hint | Amber lamp | Memory |
| Footer | Deep night | — | Faint amber | Closure |

**Placeholder rule:** The colour of the placeholder gradient should be sourced from the light description above, not from the brand palette. The placeholder is imitating light, not the brand.
